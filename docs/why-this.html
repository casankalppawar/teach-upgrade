/teach — adaptive mastery coach

Most AI tutoring is fluent forgetting.

You read a flawless explanation, nod along, and retain almost none of it — recognition dressed up as knowledge. This flips it. You do the producing, it adapts to where you are, and nothing counts as learned until you can teach it back.

Runs in any agentic CLI Your progress = plain files you own Stop & resume across months
What it adds
1

It meets you where you are

The teaching method is chosen by your level — worked examples when it's new, hard questions once you've got a foothold, teach-back at the top. It won't lecture an expert or grill a beginner on something they've never seen.

2

Encode first, then test

You build the structure — group it, connect it, map it — before you get drilled. Cramming fills a bucket with a hole in it; this patches the hole first, so what you learn actually stays.

3

It remembers — for months

A living map and log on your disk. Walk away for three weeks, come back, and it knows exactly where you stopped. Ask for a recap and it hands back the clean, distilled spine of any topic.

4

The bar is teaching it

Nothing is "done" until you can explain it to someone else — in a context you weren't taught in, including where it breaks. In-the-moment fluency is the trap; reconstruction is the proof.

5

Grounded, not guessed

The shape of the field and every fact trace back to real books and papers — sourced before it's taught, never filled in from the model's confident guesses.

6

Yours, and portable

Everything is plain Markdown you own, written to your working directory. No black-box memory, no vendor lock-in — take it to whatever agent you use.
