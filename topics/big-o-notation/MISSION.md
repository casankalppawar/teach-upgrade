# Mission: Big-O Notation & Algorithmic Complexity

## Why
Wants to stop guessing why one solution is "slow" and the other "fast" in code review and interviews — to read a function and *say*, with reasons, how it scales, and to pick the right algorithm before writing it rather than after profiling it.

## Target depth
**Able to teach it.** The interview/review use case means recognition isn't enough — needs to explain a complexity out loud and defend it. Areas that matter most: analyzing code (Area 4), common classes (Area 3), and practical judgment (Area 8). Recurrences (Area 7) to `understands` is enough; full Master-Theorem fluency is optional.

## Constraints
- ~3 short sessions a week; learns best from worked examples first, then doing it.
- Comfortable with code, rusty on the maths — go light on limit proofs, heavy on intuition + counting operations.
- No deadline; this is durable knowledge, so teach-back bar stands.
